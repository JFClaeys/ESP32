When you say the arm moves "forward" vs "backward" — which motion do you mean?

This single choice decides which MPU6050 signal we use and how reliable the direction detection is. Pick the one closest to the real gesture (tell me in the terminal).

AReach / punch

linear: hand extends forward, pulls back

Dominant signal: linear acceleration on one axis (gravity contaminates it).

cleanest signal

BArm swing about the shoulder

rotational: whole arm swings fwd/back, like marching

Dominant signal: gyroscope angular rate — signed, gravity-independent.

CForearm / wrist rotation

twist or flick at the wrist

Dominant signal: gyroscope on the roll axis.

DHand tilt (point up vs down)

orientation: where the hand is aimed

Dominant signal: accelerometer tilt vs gravity — easy, but it's posture, not motion.

Why this matters: your current code uses sqrt(x²+y²+z²) (the acceleration magnitude). That value is the same whether you move forward or backward — it throws direction away, and it sits at ~9.8 (gravity) when still. So whatever you pick above, we'll need a signed signal, and B/C point straight at the gyroscope you already have wired.
