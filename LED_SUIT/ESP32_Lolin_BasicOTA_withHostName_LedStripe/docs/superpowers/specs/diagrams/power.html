Powering it: LiPo 3.7 V → ESP32 (3.3 V) + 25× WS2812

Your Lolin C3 Pico already makes its own 3.3 V from the battery (onboard regulator + charger), so the ESP32 is handled either way. The real decision is the LED rail: boost the battery up to a steady 5 V, or drive the strip straight off the raw battery. Legend: power GND data

recommended for a costume

15 V boost rail + level shifter

Pros

  • WS2812 at nominal 5V: full, true colors
  • Brightness stays constant as battery drains
  • Rock-solid data at 5V via cheap 74AHCT125

Cons

  • Two extra parts (boost + shifter)
  • Boost must handle ~1.3A peak; small idle draw
  • Slightly more bulk/heat on the wrist
minimal parts

2Direct drive off the battery (no boost)

Pros

  • Fewest parts, lightest, most efficient
  • No level shifter (3.3V data is plenty at 3.7V)
  • Less to fail; longer runtime

Cons

  • LEDs dim + colors shift as pack sags to ~3.4V
  • Near empty (<3.4V) some WS2812 glitch/flicker
  • Brightness varies with charge state

⚠ Two things in your current diagram worth fixing either way

1. Low-side switching can back-power the strip through the data pin. Your IRLZ44N cuts the strip's GND. When it's off but the ESP32 still holds the data line at 3.3V, current sneaks in through the WS2812's data-pin clamp diode — you get a faint glow, leakage, or odd latch-up during "off"/sleep. Fixes: drive data LOW (and the strip clear) before cutting power, and/or switch the high side (V+) instead of GND. You already set brightness 0 before sleep — also push a black strip.show() and idle the pin.

2. IRLZ44N is huge for a wearable. It's a TO-220 power MOSFET rated for tens of amps. For ~1.3A you can use a tiny logic-level part (e.g. AO3400 / DMG2305 in SOT-23) — far lighter on the gauntlet, still logic-level gate-friendly. Keep your 10kΩ pull on the gate.
