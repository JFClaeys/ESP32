Replace the 4N35 with a logic-level N-MOSFET (low-side switch)

This is what MOSFET_CTRL_PIN (GPIO21) was always meant to drive. Same firmware: HIGH = LEDs powered, LOW = LEDs cut. The MOSFET carries the full strip current effortlessly; the GPIO only charges its gate. V+ GND data gate signal

  • Drain → strip GND; Source → battery GND; Gate → GPIO21 through ~220 Ω, with a 10 kΩ pulldown gate→GND (keeps it OFF while the ESP32 boots / sleeps).
  • Any logic-level N-FET works: AO3400 (SOT-23, tiny) or 2N7002 for low current; the IRLZ44N would also work but it's bulky overkill.
  • This is low-side switching, which means the data-pin back-power gotcha applies: before deep sleep, blank the strip (strip.clear(); strip.show();) and idle the data pin, then drive GPIO21 LOW. Otherwise a faint glow leaks through DIN while "off".

Optional upgrade: high-side P-FET (no glow at all)

If the residual glow bothers you, switch the strip's V+ with a P-channel MOSFET load-switch instead. It fully isolates the strip when off (no back-power path), at the cost of one extra small NPN/N-FET to drive the P-FET gate. For a first build, the low-side N-FET above + blanking the data line is simpler and good enough.
