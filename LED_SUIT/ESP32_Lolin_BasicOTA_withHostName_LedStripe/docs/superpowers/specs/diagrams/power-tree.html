Power tree — only ONE thing differs between the two builds

Both feed the ESP32 the same way (battery → the board's own regulator). The only difference is the LED branch: a boost to 5 V, or a straight tap of the raw battery. Every part shown is available in through-hole.

full brightness

Option 1 — boost the LED branch to 5 V

  • Adds one converter (boost) + a DIP-14 74AHCT125. Both THT.
  • Shifter runs on the 5V rail only — reads your 3.3V signal fine.
  • Constant brightness/true color as the battery drains.
  • Battery must source ~2A peaks (through the boost) → use a decent-C 1S pack.
simplest

Option 2 — LEDs straight off the battery

  • No converter, no shifter. Only the battery + the MOSFET switch.
  • 3.3V data is fine into a 3.7V strip (threshold ≈2.6V).
  • LEDs dim & colors shift as the pack sags; possible flicker <3.4V.
  • Lightest, most efficient, longest runtime, fewest failure points.

The MOSFET — you were right to reach for through-hole

Forget my SMD suggestion. For THT, your original IRLZ44N is genuinely the correct kind of part — TO-220 through-hole, logic-level gate (turns on fully at 3.3V), handles many times 1.3A. The only "mistake" earlier was replacing it with the 4N35 opto in the power path. So the fix is simply: go back to the IRLZ44N (or a lighter TO-220 logic-level part like IRLB8721 / FQP30N06L if you want less bulk).

Trap to avoid: the cheap blue IRF520 MOSFET modules are not logic-level — they won't fully turn on at 3.3V. If you buy a module, make sure it's an IRLZ44 / D4184 (logic-level) one.
